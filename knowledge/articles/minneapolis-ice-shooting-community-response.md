# What Trump and ICE Are Doing in Minneapolis Is Obvious. They Don't Seem to Grasp What's Happening Instead.

**Source:** [Slate](https://slate.com/news-and-politics/2026/01/trump-ice-agent-shooting-minneapolis-renee-good.html)
**Author:** Aymann Ismail
**Published:** January 16, 2026

## Summary

After an ICE agent was shot in Minneapolis during an enforcement operation, the Trump administration used it as justification for aggressive federal action. However, rather than intimidating the community, it sparked grassroots organizing with residents forming volunteer networks to protect vulnerable neighbors. The article documents how ordinary Minneapolis residents responded with solidarity and mutual aid rather than fear.

## Key Points

- ICE agent Renee Good was shot during an immigration enforcement operation in early December
- Trump administration used the incident to justify expanded federal presence and threatened use of the Insurrection Act
- Minneapolis community organized volunteer networks ("Signal network C", "Neighbors Helping Neighbors") to warn and protect immigrants
- Volunteers monitor ICE activity and dispatch warnings, averaging 3-5 alerts per day
- Response mirrors organizing that emerged after George Floyd's murder in 2020
- The community's decentralized resistance demonstrates resilience against federal intimidation tactics

## Why It Matters

The article showcases how authoritarian tactics can backfire by galvanizing community solidarity. Minneapolis residents' response illustrates grassroots organizing in action - neighbors protecting neighbors through distributed networks rather than top-down coordination. It's a case study in community resilience and mutual aid under pressure.
